COPPA (Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act)
COPPA gives parents control over the collection of personal information from children under 13. The Federal Trade Commission enforces the COPPA Rule to protect children’s privacy and safety online.
We do not market to children under 13.

Fair Information Practices
The Fair Information Practices Principles form the foundation of privacy law in the US and worldwide. They outline crucial principles for data protection.

In line with these principles, we will take the following actions if a data breach occurs:
- We will notify affected users via email within 7 business days.

We also adhere to the Individual Redress Principle, which grants individuals the right to legally pursue data processors who fail to comply with privacy laws. This principle ensures recourse through courts or government agencies for non-compliance.

CAN-SPAM Act
The CAN-SPAM Act outlines the rules for commercial emails, allowing recipients to stop unwanted emails and enforcing penalties for violations.
We collect email addresses to:
- Send information, respond to inquiries, and address requests or questions.

In line with CAN-SPAM, we agree to:
- Not use misleading subjects or email addresses.

If you wish to unsubscribe from future emails, please email us, and we will promptly remove you from all correspondence.
